# Intensity cross-check (Sentinel-1 backscatter)

Generated by `classify_intensity.py` on 2026-06-23.

This is a second independent attempt to confirm the SAR damage signal, this time from the radar itself rather than from optical imagery. The interferometric coherence measures decorrelation, which the Sahel rains drive down over the whole scene. The backscatter intensity (sigma0) is an incoherent channel: it reacts to the physical structure of a surface, standing walls versus rubble, and barely responds to seasonal moisture. If destruction were radiometrically visible, the intensity should change exactly where the coherence collapses.

For each epoch the mean sigma0 per 50 m cell is taken over the epoch's scenes, per polarisation, and the change against the pre-conflict reference E1 is the dual-pol mean log-ratio `LR = 10 * log10(sigma0_epoch / sigma0_E1)`. Collapse can raise or lower backscatter, so the cross-check is unsigned and tests `|LR|`. The significance threshold is calibrated on the unbuilt reference cells: `|LR|` must exceed `INTENSITY_CHANGE_SIGMA` (2.0) robust standard deviations (1.4826 * MAD) of the unbuilt log-ratio for that epoch, so ordinary seasonal backscatter variation on bare ground is absorbed into the threshold.

## Result: intensity does not independently confirm the coherence extent

| Epoch | Threshold `\|LR\|` | Built-up cells with intensity change | Coherence-affected confirmed by intensity |
| :--- | ---: | ---: | ---: |
| E2a | 1.34 dB | 921 / 13,152 (7 %) | 9 % |
| E2b | 2.84 dB | 363 / 13,152 (3 %) | 2 % |
| E3  | 4.07 dB | 154 / 13,152 (1 %) | 1 % |

The Pearson correlation between the drift-corrected coherence loss and the absolute intensity log-ratio over built-up cells (epoch E2b) is **-0.11**, effectively zero. The two channels are nearly independent: only 15 to 18 % of the cells with an intensity change are also coherence-affected, and only 1 to 9 % of the coherence-affected cells show a matching intensity change. The log-ratio distribution over built-up cells sits almost on top of the unbuilt reference distribution, so the channel barely separates damaged ground from bare soil.

## Why a false-positive filter is not the headline

The intent was to use the rain-robust intensity as a false-positive filter: keep a coherence-flagged cell only when the backscatter also changed. The filter is implemented (`damagef_<epoch>` in the output GeoPackage), but applied as the headline it collapses the drift-corrected extent to 1.1 % (E2a), 0.5 % (E2b) and 0.2 % (E3). That is not a credible damage estimate. It is the sensitivity floor of a channel that is largely blind to this building material, the same finding as the optical cross-validation and for the same physical reason.

El Geneina is built from mud brick. A destroyed building collapses into rubble that scatters radar much like the surrounding bare soil, so most genuine collapses produce only a weak, inconsistently signed backscatter change. Coherence instead responds to the geometric disturbance of the surface regardless of its scattering strength, which is why it carries the damage signal where intensity does not.

The intensity-filtered numbers therefore read as a strict lower bound that is biased far too low, not as a correction to the headline. The drift-corrected dual-pol figures (E2a 12 %, E2b 21 %, E3 24 % affected) stand. This cross-check adds a second, radar-internal line of evidence that single-pixel-scale incoherent change detection fails on mud-brick construction, which is the methodological case for using coherence change detection here in the first place.
